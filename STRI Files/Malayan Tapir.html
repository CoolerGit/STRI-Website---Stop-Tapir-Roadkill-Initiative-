WHY TAPIRS CROSS ROADS

In search of food (leaves, twigs, barks) and water supply

To look for partners so that they can mate

To escape fragmentation of their habitat

To move between seasonal habitats (Dry and wet conditions)

To escape from predators, natural and humans inclusive

To find a suitable place to rest and relax

WHY ARE TAPIRS ROADKILLED

Vehicles travelling over the speed limit

Abundance of blind spots on roads

Low visibility for drivers at night

Camouflage at night due to their black and white colour

Climatic conditions like fog and rain hinder vision for motorists

Effects of Tapir Roadkill

Causality / injury sustained by the Tapir

Causality / injury sustained by the chauffeur

Severe vehicle damage and expensive repair costs

Laborious process of cleaning up the roadkill

Unpleasant sight for commuters

May result in traffic congestion

Statistics

1. There is an increase in Tapir roadkills during the dry season as they traverse in search of water.


2. There are the highest number of roadkills in the state of Terengganu.


3. A total of 115 incidents from 2006 to 2019 have been reported.


4. The highest number of Tapir roadkills were recorded in 2017.


5. There is an increase in the number of Tapir roadkills as the forest area decreases.

SLIDE TO PROTECT ME!

Civilian Action

1. Take extra precaution while driving through forest reserves at night when the visibility is low.

2. If youâ€™re involved in a collision with a Tapir, stop to check its well-being.

3. If the animal is alive and injured, call the Malaysian wildlife rescue service.

4. Flash your high beam at the Tapir to gain their attention and deter it away.

5. Honk at the Tapir which is about to enter the roadway to scare it off.

Government Initiatives

1. Create Tapir roadkill management strategies and collect data on roadkill reported by the public.

2. Identify hotspots for Malayan Tapir roadkill and construct crossings, such as underpasses.

3. Install Tapir warning reflectors along roads.

4. Fence vulnerable areas bordering roads or highways.

Malayan Tapir Protection

1. Setup special hotlines for civilians to report Malayan Tapir roadkills and injuries.

2. Start organisations to protect the Malayan Tapir's habitat from forest fragmentation.

3. Install more Malayan Tapir crossing signs to reduce the number of road accidents.

4. Set-up more awareness programs to inform drivers on how to avoid Tapir roadkills.
